How Parch Chat Works

This is the plain-language version. Parch chat does not use email logins for chat identity. Your browser creates your chat identity, and only invited people can read messages in spaces they are part of.

1) Your Chat Identity

The first time you open chat, your browser creates your identity. Think of it like your private chat passport. No central chat password is needed.

  • You get a public key you can share for invites.
  • You keep your private identity data on your device.
  • You can export/import identity to move to another browser or device.

2) Joining a Host

When you connect, the host checks that you really control your identity. After that, you are signed in for chat on that host.

  • No email + password step for chat.
  • Your chosen fallback username can be sent on first join.
  • Each host can keep its own username for you.

3) Invites

Invites are sent to your public key, not your email address. Share your public key with a space admin to be invited.

  • Admin adds your public key to invite you.
  • You accept the invite in your account dashboard.
  • You then appear as a member in that space.

4) Message Privacy

Messages are encrypted before they leave your browser. The relay and host can deliver/store messages, but cannot read message text.

  • Only intended members can open message content.
  • Changed/tampered message payloads are rejected.
  • People invited later do not automatically read old messages.

Important: back up your exported identity file if you want continuity across browsers/devices. If you lose your private identity data, you lose access to old encrypted chat history tied to that identity.
